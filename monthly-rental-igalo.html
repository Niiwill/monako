Location

Igalo, Herceg Novi — Inside the Bay of Kotor

Igalo sits at the very entrance to the Bay of Kotor (Boka Kotorska) — a UNESCO-listed fjord-like bay that splits the southern Adriatic coast. The apartment's west-facing balcony looks directly across the bay. On clear mornings (roughly 240 per year, by local meteorological records), the mountains of the Orjen massif rise behind the town and the bay is mirror-flat before the coastal wind picks up at midday.

Unlike the beach-resort towns further south (Budva, 50 km; Bar, 90 km; Ulcinj, 110 km), Herceg Novi and Igalo are year-round residential towns. That distinction matters for monthly renters: markets stay open, restaurants serve locals, bus lines run on schedule, and prices don't double in June.

Institut Igalo — wellness as part of daily life

The Special Hospital for Medical Rehabilitation "Institut Dr Simo Milošević" — universally known as Institut Igalo — is five minutes' walk from the apartment. For over 60 years it has treated guests from across Europe and Russia using natural peloid mud and mineral-rich Bay of Kotor seawater. Monthly residents of Igalo can access its thalassotherapy pools, massages, and outdoor sea pools at local rates significantly below visitor prices.

Getting to Igalo from the airport

Dubrovnik Airport (DBV, Croatia) is typically the cheapest gateway for Western European arrivals — 35 km from Igalo, around 35–40 minutes by taxi (€25–€35). Tivat Airport (TIV) is 50 km / 50–60 minutes. Podgorica Airport (TGD) is 90 km. The Lepetane–Kamenari bay ferry (runs every 15–20 minutes in season) cuts the coastal road drive from Tivat to Igalo by 30 minutes. Private airport transfer can be arranged on request.

The Bay of Kotor ferry — practical travel tip

The Kamenari–Lepetane car ferry is one of the most useful pieces of transport infrastructure in Montenegro. It crosses the narrowest point of Boka Bay in 5 minutes, connecting Igalo and Herceg Novi to Tivat and the southern coast without the 30-minute coastal drive. The ferry runs daily from early morning to late evening, year-round, for a nominal fee. Knowing this route halves the effective distance to Tivat, Budva, and Kotor.
