Why choose Igalo for a longer stay?

Unlike Budva or Bar, Igalo and Herceg Novi have a distinct character — urban life without the crowds, cultural offerings, lush greenery and Mediterranean architecture. The town is proud of Institut Igalo, a renowned rehabilitation centre that attracts visitors from all over the world, giving the area an international feel even off-season.

The coastal walk from Igalo towards the old town of Herceg Novi is one of the most beautiful pedestrian routes on the Adriatic. The old town with its Venetian fortresses, flower-lined promenades and views over the bay — all of this is just a short walk from your apartment.

Climate and weather off-season

Herceg Novi is considered one of the sunniest places on the Adriatic — with around 240 sunny days per year. Off-season temperatures are mild (10–20°C in winter months), the sea is calm, and the town breathes authentic Mediterranean life without the tourist buzz. Perfect for those seeking a healthier pace of living.

About the destination

Igalo and Herceg Novi — A Destination for an Extended Stay

When travellers think of Montenegro, the summer Riviera typically comes to mind — Budva, Sveti Stefan, Ulcinj. However, a monthly apartment rental in Igalo reveals a completely different Montenegro — the off-season one, authentic, peaceful and extraordinarily beautiful.

Herceg Novi — the city of flowers and sun

Herceg Novi (to which Igalo administratively belongs) carries the nickname "the city of sun and flowers" — and rightly so. Mimosa, bougainvillea and oleander line the streets and terraces for a good half of the year. The old town with its Venetian fortresses and windows overlooking the bay ranks among the most beautiful historic settings on the Adriatic.

For those who rent an apartment on a monthly basis, this becomes an everyday backdrop — not a destination you travel to, but a space in which you live.

Institut Igalo — a world-renowned health centre

Just five minutes' walk from the apartment stands the famous Special Hospital for Medical Rehabilitation "Institut Dr Simo Milošević" — better known simply as Institut Igalo. For decades it has attracted guests from all over the world thanks to its natural peloid mud and mineral-rich seawater. Residents of Igalo have the privilege of having this wellness resort literally around the corner.

Nature and active lifestyle

The surroundings of Herceg Novi are ideal for those who love an active way of life. Mount Orjen above the town is one of the greenest mountains in the Balkans, with numerous hiking trails and canyons. Along the coast, cycling and walking paths offer a daily dose of physical activity without leaving the municipality.

During the winter months — when monthly rentals are most sought after — the mountain sometimes offers snow, while the sea is barely an hour's drive away. This contrast of natural environments is unique to Igalo and the surrounding area.

Transport and connections with the world

Igalo is well connected with the rest of Montenegro and the Balkans. Tivat Airport is around an hour and a half's drive away, and Podgorica Airport is about two hours. Bus lines directly connect Herceg Novi with Dubrovnik (Croatia), Split, Sarajevo and Belgrade — which is practical for those who need to travel occasionally.

For getting around the municipality, the bicycle (included in the rental price) is the ideal mode of transport — everything is flat along the coast, and the distance between Igalo and Herceg Novi town centre is just two to three kilometres.
